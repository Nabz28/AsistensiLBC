  paper. It is referred to below as **"2022"** (the user's file name) but it is really

Curve key: AD = aggregate demand, SRAS / LRAS = short/long-run aggregate supply,
DLF/SLF = demand/supply of loanable funds, NCO = net-capital-outflow curve,
SRPC/LRPC = short/long-run Phillips curve, MS/MD = money supply/demand (liquidity
preference). "3-panel" = loanable-funds + NCO + foreign-currency-exchange (FX) diagram.

## 1) Per-year graph/diagram questions

### 2019 (essay paper; "gunakan diagram/grafik bila perlu")

- **S1c — Fiscal expansion → budget deficit (3-panel, Week 8).** Draw the open-economy
  loanable-funds + NCO + FX response to a rise in government spending / budget deficit.
  Correct direction: DLF demand up → real r up → domestic investment down → NCO falls
  → supply of domestic currency in FX falls → **real exchange rate appreciates**, NX
  falls. (key cites Mankiw budget-deficit case; no drawn key.)
- **S1d — Capital flight (Asian crisis 1997/98), 3-panel (Week 8).** Investors pull
  assets out of Indonesia. Correct: NCO curve shifts right → DLF (demand) rises → real
  r rises → at the same time supply of rupiah in FX rises → **rupiah real exchange rate
  depreciates**; quantity of loanable funds & domestic investment fall. (key cites
  Mankiw Mexico case.)
- **S2 (intro) — Why AD slopes down / SRAS slopes up (Weeks 9).** Explain + (the part-a/b
  below ask to draw). Key gives wealth / interest-rate / exchange-rate effects (AD) and
  sticky-wage / sticky-price / misperceptions (SRAS).
- **S2a — Fall in global demand for Asgard exports → AD shift (Week 9).** Draw AD-AS,
  open economy, no intervention, SR and LR. Correct: **AD shifts left** → SR: P and Y
  both fall; LR: SRAS shifts right as expectations adjust, output returns to natural
  rate at a lower price level.
- **S2b — Rise in production costs → SRAS shift / supply shock (Week 9).** Correct:
  **SRAS shifts left** → SR: P up, Y down (stagflation); unemployment above natural;
  actual price > expected price.
- **S3a — Liquidity preference, two events (Week 10).** Draw money market + AD effect.
  (i) BI raises the discount rate → **MS shifts left** → r up → AD left.
  (ii) Households spend more for Idul Fitri → **MD shifts right** → r up.
- **S4a — SR vs LR Phillips-curve trade-off (Week 11).** Draw downward-sloping SRPC and
  vertical LRPC at the natural rate.
- **S4b — Expansionary monetary policy, AD-AS + Phillips (Weeks 10/11).** Correct: AD
  right → SR: Y up, unemployment down, inflation up (move up-left along SRPC); LR: SRAS
  left / move back to LRPC, output back to natural rate, only inflation permanently
  higher.
- **S4c — Expected inflation shifts SRPC under expansionary money (Week 11).** Correct:
  rising expected inflation **shifts SRPC up/right**; SR gain in employment is temporary,
  LR returns to natural rate at higher inflation.
